When Compliance Fails: Eye-Opening Incidents in GRC (Part 2)

Industry: Cross-Industry Year: 2023 Type: Compliance Failures

This case study continues our examination of significant compliance failures across various industries and the costly consequences of inadequate Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) frameworks. These examples offer valuable lessons for organizations looking to strengthen their compliance programs.

Volkswagen Emissions Scandal

In 2015, it was discovered that Volkswagen had installed "defeat devices" in 11 million diesel vehicles worldwide that could detect when the cars were being tested and change performance to improve results.

The Compliance Failure: The scandal revealed profound corporate governance issues, including a culture that prioritized engineering prowess and market dominance over ethical considerations and compliance with environmental regulations.

The Consequence: Volkswagen faced penalties and settlements exceeding $30 billion globally. The company's stock price plummeted, and its reputation suffered immensely. Several executives faced criminal charges, and the company was forced to undergo a comprehensive restructuring of its governance and compliance functions.

Boeing 737 MAX Crisis

In 2018 and 2019, two Boeing 737 MAX aircraft crashed, killing 346 people. Investigations revealed serious flaws in the aircraft's Maneuvering Characteristics Augmentation System (MCAS) and insufficient pilot training on the new system.

The Compliance Failure: Internal documents revealed that Boeing employees had concerns about the safety of the 737 MAX but faced pressure to meet deadlines and control costs. The company's relationship with the FAA was also called into question, with allegations that Boeing had too much influence over the certification process.

The Consequence: The 737 MAX was grounded worldwide for nearly two years. Boeing faced over $20 billion in direct costs, countless lawsuits, and irreparable damage to its reputation for safety. The crisis led to a complete overhaul of Boeing's safety culture, leadership changes, and increased regulatory scrutiny of aircraft certification processes.

Goldman Sachs and the 1MDB Scandal

Goldman Sachs faced scrutiny for its role in helping Malaysia's sovereign wealth fund, 1Malaysia Development Berhad (1MDB), raise $6.5 billion, much of which was allegedly misappropriated by government officials and their associates.

The Compliance Failure: Goldman Sachs failed to adequately address red flags in its due diligence process. The bank's compliance systems did not effectively detect or prevent the involvement of its bankers in a scheme that allegedly involved bribery of foreign officials.

The Consequence: Goldman Sachs agreed to pay nearly $3 billion to resolve investigations by authorities in the United States, United Kingdom, Singapore, and Hong Kong. Additionally, the bank's Malaysian subsidiary pleaded guilty to violating the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act. The scandal led to significant changes in Goldman's compliance procedures and a renewed focus on reputational risk management.

Common Patterns in Major Compliance Failures

Across these cases, several common patterns emerge:

  • Culture Over Compliance: In each case, organizational culture prioritized financial performance, growth, or engineering excellence over ethical considerations and regulatory compliance.
  • Inadequate Risk Assessment: Organizations failed to properly identify, assess, and mitigate significant compliance risks in their operations.
  • Warning Signs Ignored: Internal warnings or red flags were frequently dismissed or downplayed due to conflicting priorities.
  • Weaknesses in Oversight: Boards and senior management failed to provide adequate oversight of organizational activities and compliance programs.
  • Short-term Focus: Decision-making prioritized short-term financial or competitive gains over long-term sustainability and compliance.

Implementing Effective GRC Programs

Organizations can protect themselves by implementing robust GRC programs that incorporate:

  • A strong ethical culture where compliance is seen as everyone's responsibility
  • Comprehensive risk assessment processes that identify and address emerging risks
  • Clear escalation paths for compliance concerns raised by employees
  • Regular, independent assessment of compliance programs
  • Meaningful board and senior management oversight of compliance matters
  • Alignment of incentives with ethical and compliant behavior

These case studies demonstrate that the costs of compliance failures far exceed the investments required to maintain effective compliance programs. Organizations that learn from these cautionary tales can strengthen their GRC frameworks to avoid similar pitfalls.
