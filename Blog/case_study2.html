PwC fined £2.9m over audit of Sanjeev Gupta's Wyelands Bank

Industry: Financial Services Year: 2023 Type: Audit Failure

PwC has been fined £2.9m by the Financial Reporting Council (FRC) over its audit of Wyelands Bank, which was owned by the steel magnate Sanjeev Gupta and was later ordered to repay depositors by the Bank of England.

The fine is the latest development in the saga surrounding Gupta's GFG Alliance, a collection of companies that included the bank as well as Liberty Steel, a major UK steelmaker.

Details of the Case

The FRC found that PwC and the audit engagement partner, Patrick Sarch, had failed to properly challenge Wyelands' management on the recoverability of loans. They also failed to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence and to adequately document their audit work.
