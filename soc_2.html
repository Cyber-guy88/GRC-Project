Choose Your Report Type

Decide between SOC 2 Type I and Type II reports based on your organization's needs.

SOC 2 Type I

"Like a photograph of your security controls"

  • Focus: Design of controls at a specific point in time
  • Timeframe: Snapshot in time
  • Scope: Less comprehensive
  • Cost: Less expensive
  • Purpose: Demonstrates that controls are properly designed

SOC 2 Type II

"Like a documentary of your security controls"

  • Focus: Design and operational effectiveness of controls
  • Timeframe: Period of time (3-12 months)
  • Scope: More comprehensive
  • Cost: More expensive
  • Purpose: Demonstrates both design and operational effectiveness

Key Difference

The main difference between Type 1 and Type 2 reports is the assessment period and depth. Type 1 assesses controls at a specific moment, while Type 2 evaluates both design and effectiveness over an extended period.

Making Your Decision

Consider your organization's:

  • Budget constraints
  • Time requirements
  • Client requirements
  • Compliance needs

Conduct a Risk Assessment

Understanding Risk Assessment

A vital step in your SOC 2 compliance journey to safeguard your information assets. Think of it as a comprehensive safety check for your valuable data.

1. Risk Identification

Identify potential risks to your data and systems

Example

Potential unauthorized access to customer database containing sensitive information

2. Impact Assessment

Evaluate the potential severity and consequences of each identified risk

  • Assess impact severity
  • Determine occurrence likelihood
  • Prioritize risks based on analysis

3. Mitigation Strategy

Develop comprehensive risk mitigation strategies and security controls

  • Create protective measures
  • Implement security controls
  • Establish monitoring procedures

Control Mapping and Gap Analysis

Understanding Gap Analysis

A thorough assessment to align your internal control environment with Trust Services Criteria (TSC).

Control Mapping

  • Map existing controls to TSC requirements
  • Document control objectives and procedures
  • Identify control ownership and responsibilities

Gap Identification

Example

If TSC requires system activity monitoring, assess whether current logging controls meet these requirements

Conduct Internal Audit

Internal Audit Overview

A proactive approach to ensure the safety and protection of your business assets before the formal SOC 2 audit.

Security Verification

Example

Regular checks on customer data protection measures against unauthorized access

Key Benefits

  • Early risk identification
  • Stakeholder confidence
  • Improved security posture
  • Better preparation for formal audit
