What is SOC 2?

SOC 2 (System and Organization Controls 2) is a framework developed by the American Institute of CPAs (AICPA) that defines criteria for managing customer data based on five "trust service principles" — security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy. It is specifically designed for service providers storing customer data in the cloud.

Unlike other compliance frameworks that focus on specific industries or data types, SOC 2 is adaptable and allows organizations to select the criteria most relevant to their business practices and customer promises.

Trust Service Criteria

Security

Protection against unauthorized access, both physical and logical. This covers system protection against unauthorized access, data breaches, theft, and other security vulnerabilities.

  • Access controls
  • Firewalls and security monitoring
  • Vulnerability management
  • Incident response planning

Availability

Systems are available for operation and use as committed or agreed upon. This addresses system uptime, performance, and reliability.

  • Business continuity planning
  • Disaster recovery procedures
  • Performance monitoring
  • Redundancy measures

Processing Integrity

System processing is complete, accurate, timely, and authorized. This ensures that data processing operates as intended without errors or delays.

  • Quality assurance processes
  • Process monitoring
  • Error handling procedures
  • Transaction validation

Confidentiality

Information designated as confidential is protected as committed or agreed. This covers sensitive business information, intellectual property, and personal data.

  • Data classification
  • Encryption protocols
  • Data retention policies
  • Confidentiality agreements

Privacy

Personal information is collected, used, retained, disclosed, and disposed of in accordance with the entity's privacy notice. This addresses compliance with privacy laws and regulations.

  • Privacy notice management
  • Consent mechanisms
  • Data subject rights
  • Personal data handling

SOC 2 Report Types

Type I

Point-in-Time Assessment

"A snapshot of your security controls"

The Type I report assesses the design of security controls at a specific point in time.

  • Focus: Control design suitability
  • Timeframe: Single point in time
  • Verification: Confirms controls are properly designed
  • Audit Period: One day
  • Complexity: Less complex, faster to obtain
  • Ideal for: First-time SOC 2 compliers
Type II

Historical Assessment

"A movie of your security controls"

The Type II report assesses both the design and operating effectiveness of controls over a period of time.

  • Focus: Control design and operational effectiveness
  • Timeframe: Extended period (3-12 months)
  • Verification: Tests whether controls operate effectively
  • Audit Period: Minimum 6 months recommended
  • Complexity: More complex, higher assurance
  • Ideal for: Mature security programs

Compliance Process

1

Scoping & Planning

Define the scope of your SOC 2 audit by selecting relevant trust criteria and identifying systems and data within scope.

  • Select applicable trust criteria
  • Identify systems in scope
  • Define control boundaries
  • Select an audit firm
2

Gap Assessment

Evaluate your current security controls against SOC 2 requirements to identify gaps and areas for improvement.

  • Review existing controls
  • Identify control gaps
  • Develop remediation plan
  • Establish control framework
3

Control Implementation

Implement and refine controls to address identified gaps and meet SOC 2 requirements.

  • Develop new policies and procedures
  • Implement technical controls
  • Train personnel
  • Document control activities
4

Readiness Assessment

Conduct a pre-audit review to determine if controls are operating effectively before the formal audit.

  • Test control effectiveness
  • Gather evidence
  • Address final issues
  • Prepare for audit
5

Formal Audit

Work with an independent CPA firm to conduct the official SOC 2 audit and assessment.

  • Auditor control testing
  • Evidence collection
  • Audit interviews
  • Results evaluation
6

Reporting & Remediation

Receive the audit report, address any identified issues, and share results with relevant stakeholders.

  • Report assessment
  • Address exceptions
  • Share with stakeholders
  • Continuous improvement

Benefits of SOC 2 Compliance

Competitive Advantage

Differentiate your business by demonstrating commitment to security and customer data protection.

Enhanced Security

Implement comprehensive security controls that protect your organization and customer data.

Risk Reduction

Identify and mitigate security risks before they lead to data breaches or service disruptions.

Customer Trust

Build stronger customer relationships based on verified security practices and transparency.

Streamlined Due Diligence

Accelerate sales cycles by providing a recognized security attestation to potential clients.

Regulatory Alignment

Align with multiple regulatory requirements through a single comprehensive framework.

Implementation Challenges

Resource-Intensive

Type II audits require months of preparation, continuous monitoring, and significant resource allocation.

Dynamic Controls

Requires continuous monitoring and updates to ensure controls remain effective as technology and threats evolve.

Cost Considerations

Audits can cost $30,000–$100,000+, depending on organization size, complexity, and scope of the assessment.

SOC 2 vs. Other Frameworks

Framework Focus Key Characteristics
SOC 1 Financial reporting controls Focuses specifically on controls relevant to financial reporting (e.g., billing systems)
SOC 3 Public-facing report A simplified, shareable version of SOC 2 without sensitive technical details
NIST CSF Risk management Broader risk management framework versus SOC 2's service-provider focus
ISO 27001 Information security Internationally recognized but more prescriptive compared to SOC 2's flexibility

Practical Tips for Compliance

Scope Selection

Align Trust Services Criteria selection with customer expectations and business requirements.

Automate Controls

Use tools like Vanta or Sprinto to streamline evidence collection and continuous monitoring.

Staff Training

Ensure all employees understand policies and security practices through regular training, such as phishing simulations.

Annual Renewal

Remember that SOC 2 reports are valid for approximately 12 months and require regular renewal.

Key Takeaways

Not a Certification

SOC 2 is an attestation with no formal "pass/fail" designation; it provides a qualified opinion on control effectiveness.

Market Demand

Critical for SaaS providers, cloud services, and any organization processing customer data, particularly in enterprise sales cycles.

Flexibility

Organizations can tailor controls to their unique systems and business requirements while meeting the Trust Services Criteria.
