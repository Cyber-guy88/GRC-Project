Governance

  • Leadership and Oversight: Establishing a clear organizational structure with defined roles and responsibilities, including the Board of Directors, executive management, and committees. This ensures proper decision-making and accountability in cybersecurity matters.
  • Strategic Direction: Setting long-term goals and objectives that align with the organization's mission and values. This includes defining cybersecurity priorities and resource allocation.
  • Policy Framework: Developing and implementing policies, procedures, and guidelines that govern operations and decision-making. This creates a structured approach to managing cybersecurity risks.
  • Stakeholder Engagement: Involving stakeholders in governance processes to ensure their interests and concerns are considered. This promotes transparency and buy-in for cybersecurity initiatives.
  • Framework: COBIT, COSO, ITIL, ISO/IEC 38500

Risk Management

  • Risk Identification: Identifying potential risks that could impact the organization's objectives, including financial, operational, strategic, and reputational risks. This includes threats like cyberattacks, data breaches, and system failures.
  • Risk Assessment: Evaluating the likelihood and impact of identified risks to prioritize and address them effectively. This helps in allocating resources to the most critical security concerns.
  • Risk Mitigation: Implementing controls and measures such as risk transfer (insurance), risk avoidance, or risk reduction strategies. This includes technical controls, security policies, and employee training.
  • Risk Monitoring: Continuously monitoring and reviewing risks to ensure mitigation measures remain effective and identify emerging risks. This includes real-time threat detection and response capabilities.
  • Framework: NIST CSF, ISO 27001, ISO 31000, NIST AI RMF, NIST SP 800-53, NIST SP 800-12, NIST SP 800-14, NIST SP 800-26

Compliance

  • Regulatory Compliance: Ensuring adherence to laws, regulations, and industry standards, such as data protection, anti-corruption, and health and safety regulations. This includes cybersecurity-specific regulations like GDPR and HIPAA.
  • Internal Compliance: Developing and enforcing internal policies and codes of conduct to ensure ethical behavior and adherence to organizational standards. This includes security policies and acceptable use guidelines.
  • Auditing and Reporting: Conducting regular audits and assessments to verify compliance, reporting findings to stakeholders, and implementing corrective actions. This ensures continuous improvement of security posture.
  • Framework: SOC2, HIPAA, PCI-DSS, GDPR, DPDPA, CCPA, FedRAMP, CMMC, RBI, FISMA, IT ACT
